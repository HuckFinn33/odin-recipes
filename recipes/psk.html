Pork and Sauerkraut Dinner

Description

Straight from the fatherland, your Oma's pork and sauerkraut dinner is a blitzkrieg on your taste buds. The flavors of this dish combined with merlot or cabernet, capped with your favorite apple brandy, wraps you in a embrace like the warmth of a winter fire. Each bite will have you saying auf wietersehen to your worries and guten tag to good times.

Ingredients

  • 1 (32-ounce) package sauerkraut, undrained
  • 1 medium apple, cored, cut into thin wedges
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 2 tablespoons firmly packed brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 (2- to 3-pound) boneless pork loin roast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. Combine sauerkraut, apple, onion, brown sugar and maple syrup in large bowl; set aside.
  3. Sprinkle pork roast with salt and pepper. Melt butter in 5-quart ovenproof saucepan or Dutch oven until sizzling;
    add pork roast. Cook over medium heat, turning several times, 7-9 minutes or until browned.
  4. Add sauerkraut mixture; cover. Bake 1 1/2-2 hours or until pork roast reaches internal temperature of 160 degrees F.
  5. Using slotted spoon, transfer sauerkraut to serving dish. Place pork roast over sauerkraut.
